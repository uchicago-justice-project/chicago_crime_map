Chicago Peacekeepers & Violent Crime

This map explores changes in violent crime across areas in Chicago, looking specifically at methods the city has implemented or dropped in 2025. Scroll down to explore the story.

For the first six months of 2025 Chicago has seen a 17.67% drop in violent crime compared to the first six months of 2024. This map shows the percent difference in violent crime through the first six months between 2024 and 2025 aggregated by police beats. Areas shaded in blue indicate a decrease in violent crime, while areas shaded in red indicate an increase. The deeper the shade, the larger the change.
In September 2024 Chicago cancelled their contract with Shotspotter which installed sensors all over Chicago to detect gunshots. Despite a lack of strong evidence in support of Shotspotter's efficacy some argued that the cancelation of the contract would increase crime in those areas (largely the south and west sides of Chicago). However the areas where sensors were located shown here still saw a 17.76% decrease in violent crime without those sensors being active, putting them in line with the city wide decrease.
Chicago PD also utilized hotspot analysis to determine what areas to divert more resources to. Many have cited this as a possible reason for the decreasing crime rate. The police beats with hotspots saw a decrease of 17.97% roughly the same as the overall city.
In addition to policing measures and surveilance technology, Chicago and the state of Illinois increased thier investments in violence prevention programs, in particular Peacekeepers. This program trains community members as outreach workers to prevent violence from occuring in hotspots in their neighborhoods. Areas with a Peacekeeper hotspot saw a decrease in violent crime of 20.46%, beating the citywide average.
